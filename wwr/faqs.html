  • As with all adventure sports, there is an inherent and assumed element of risk. To help ensure your safety, our guides are all certified in Wilderness First aid and all start with at least 3 years of rafting experience. The most common injuries are sunburns and injuries that occur on land.

    Everyone on commercial rafting trips wears coast guard approved life vest or personal flotation device (PFD). Many non-swimmers go on rafting trips and have a great time. Your guide will give you an in depth and engaging safety talk before your trip begins explaining what you should prepare for and do in the event that you fall out of the raft.

  • We will provide all necessary camping equipment. This includes a sleeping bag, foam pad, waterproof duffle bag, small waterproof day bag, tent and a cot, eating utensils and camping chair.

    We provided water and lemonade during the day. Coffee, tea and hot chocolate are provided during the morning and evening hours. You may bring beer, liquor and soft drinks (in reasonable amounts, 2 cans per day). You may purchase these items either the night before the trip, or on your way to the put-in. 3 meals per day, plus snacks

  • To effectively plan for temperature and weather changes in the Canyon, it's important to be prepared for both hot and dry conditions as well as cold and wet conditions. Here are some recommended clothing and items to consider:

    Clothing:

    • Rain jacket and rain pants (avoid ponchos)
    • Quick-dry shirts and shorts
    • Long-sleeve cotton shirt and pants (helps keep you cooler on hot days)
    • Button-down shirts for added sun protection on the neck
    • Light-weight cotton pants, nylon pants, or sarong
    • Swim trunks for men (can be used as shorts)
    • Polypropylene long underwear (top and bottom) if you are cold natured
    • Swim suits or quick-dry underwear and sport bras as a base layer for women
    • Quick-dry shorts and shirt (polypropylene for cold, cotton for hot) over the base layer
    • Two-piece swim suits for convenience

    Accessories and Footwear:

    • Hat with a large brim for sun protection (ensure it has a good quality strap)
    • Sunglasses with strap
    • Socks for hiking and foot sun protection
    • Sandals or water shoes (consider bringing comfortable hiking shoes if not used to sandals)

    Additional Items:

    • Water bottle
    • Small day pack for hikes
    • Moisturizing lotion
    • Headlamp or flashlight
    • Moleskin for blister prevention
    • Waterproof sunscreen and lip balm
    • Optional items: gloves, fleece jacket, towel, bandana, small pillow

    Personal Care:

    • Hygiene products (toothpaste, toothbrush, tissue, nail clippers, feminine hygiene products)
    • Extra eyeglasses or contacts
    • Biodegradable soap and shampoo

    Entertainment:

    • Camera equipment
    • Book, watercolors, journal, and pen

    When packing, consider bringing comfortable camp wear such as cotton dresses, skirts, or sarongs. You may choose to sleep in your camp wear or bring something comfortable for sleeping. Remember to adjust your packing list according to your specific needs and the duration of your stay.

  • Everything! What's not to love about whitewater rafting? Let's dive into a typical day on the river.

    Start your day in the gentle glow of the early morning, having spent the night under a breathtaking starry sky. Enjoy a delicious and filling breakfast of fresh blueberry pancakes or made-to-order omelets. Then, gather your belongings and get ready for the adventure ahead. The day is brimming with thrilling whitewater, serene stretches, invigorating hikes, fascinating stories, glimpses into history and geology, and of course, mouthwatering meals.

    Around 4 or 5 pm, we aim to reach our campsite. After setting up your cozy cot, unwind by the riverside with a captivating book or engaging company. You can explore the camp surroundings while your guides skillfully prepare a delectable dinner, such as sizzling steak and potatoes or grilled salmon. And let's not forget the pièce de résistance—dutch oven brownies that will leave an unforgettable mark on your taste buds!

    As the evening progresses, savor moments of relaxation and perhaps convince your guide to share a few more captivating stories before you retire for the night. Fall asleep under a sky adorned with more stars than you ever imagined possible!
